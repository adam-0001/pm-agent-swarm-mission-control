COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE — Focus/Productivity Tools (March 2026)

1. CLOCKWISE ($8/user/month)
- Calendar optimization and smart scheduling
- "Focus Time" auto-blocks on calendar
- Team analytics dashboard
- Strengths: Good calendar integration, team-level features
- Weaknesses: No Slack integration, no distraction blocking, purely calendar-based

2. RECLAIM.AI ($10/user/month)
- AI calendar assistant
- Auto-schedules habits, focus time, meetings
- Slack status sync
- Strengths: Smart scheduling, good Slack integration
- Weaknesses: Doesn't actually block distractions, no productivity measurement, calendar-only approach

3. FREEDOM ($8.99/month individual)
- Website and app blocker
- Scheduled focus sessions
- Cross-device blocking
- Strengths: Effective distraction blocking, simple UX
- Weaknesses: No team features, no calendar awareness, no smart triage, individual-only

4. RESCUETIME ($12/month premium)
- Automatic time tracking
- Productivity scoring
- Weekly reports
- Strengths: Good analytics, passive tracking
- Weaknesses: Reports only (no intervention), no team features, no AI intelligence, aging product

5. CENTERED ($15/month)
- Virtual coworking with AI coach
- Flow state detection via music
- Task-based focus sessions
- Strengths: Novel approach, community element
- Weaknesses: Niche, no integrations, no team/enterprise features

6. SLACK (Enterprise Grid: $12.50/user/month)
- Has built-in DND/pause notifications
- Scheduled send feature
- Huddles as meeting alternative
- Reality: Slack IS the distraction. Their DND is binary — all or nothing. No smart message triage.

KEY GAPS IN MARKET:
- NO competitor does smart message triage (urgent vs. non-urgent)
- NO competitor combines calendar + Slack + distraction blocking in one tool
- NO competitor provides team-level focus analytics with manager dashboards
- NO competitor auto-generates end-of-day status updates
- Privacy-first/local-processing approach is unexplored territory
